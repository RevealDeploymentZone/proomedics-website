About Naproxen Sodium 220mg (80ct) — Complete Product Guide

Naproxen Sodium 220mg is an OTC nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) best known by the brand name Aleve. Unlike ibuprofen which requires dosing every 4–6 hours, naproxen offers extended 8–12 hour relief with just one tablet — making it ideal for conditions like arthritis, tendinitis, and chronic back pain where sustained relief throughout the day is essential.

What Conditions Does Naproxen 220mg Treat?

Naproxen 220mg is indicated for the relief of osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, tendinitis, bursitis, gout flares, muscle aches and back pain, menstrual cramps, headache and migraine, fever reduction, and minor acute injuries. It is approved for OTC use and does not require a prescription for standard adult doses.

How Does Naproxen 220mg Work?

Like other NSAIDs, naproxen inhibits COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes to block prostaglandin production, reducing pain, fever, and inflammation. Its longer half-life compared to ibuprofen means the drug stays active in your system for 8–12 hours per dose. Onset of action typically occurs within 30–60 minutes, with peak pain relief at 1–2 hours.

Dosage and Usage Instructions

Adults and children 12 and over: Take 1 tablet (220mg) every 8 to 12 hours. If needed, a second tablet may be taken within the first hour. Do not exceed 2 tablets (440mg) in any 8–12 hour period or 3 tablets (660mg) in 24 hours. Take with food or milk to reduce stomach upset. Do not use for more than 10 days for pain or 3 days for fever without medical direction.

Who Can Use Naproxen 220mg?

Suitable for adults and children 12+ for short-term OTC use. Avoid if you have stomach ulcers, kidney disease, heart disease, or take blood thinners. Elderly patients should use with caution due to increased GI risk. Not for use in late pregnancy. Always consult your doctor for chronic pain management.

Possible Side Effects

Common side effects include stomach upset, nausea, heartburn, and headache. Less common: dizziness, ringing in ears. Serious risks (rare with short-term OTC use): GI bleeding, kidney issues, cardiovascular effects. Take with food or antacids to reduce stomach irritation. Not recommended for long-term use without medical supervision.

Frequently Asked Questions — Naproxen 220mg

How long does naproxen 220mg last?

Naproxen provides 8–12 hours of relief per dose — significantly longer than Ibuprofen (4–6 hours) or acetaminophen. This makes it ideal for chronic pain conditions like arthritis where consistent coverage is needed. Source: MedlinePlus: Naproxen.

Is naproxen stronger than ibuprofen?

Naproxen and Ibuprofen are both NSAIDs with similar effectiveness, but naproxen has a longer duration. Naproxen 220mg OTC is equivalent in strength to about 200–400mg of ibuprofen. For quick short-term relief, ibuprofen may act faster; for all-day coverage, naproxen is preferred.

Can I take naproxen every day?

For short-term use (under 10 days for pain, 3 days for fever), daily naproxen is generally safe for healthy adults. Long-term daily use increases risk of stomach ulcers, cardiovascular events, and kidney issues. Always consult a healthcare provider for chronic use. See FDA NSAID Safety Warning.

Who should not take naproxen?

Naproxen is not recommended if you have kidney disease, stomach ulcers, heart disease, or take blood thinners. Do not use in the third trimester of pregnancy. Not suitable for children under 12 without medical advice.

What is the difference between naproxen and Aleve?

Aleve is the brand name; naproxen sodium 220mg is the generic active ingredient. Generic naproxen 220mg is bioequivalent to Aleve and provides the same 8–12 hour relief at a lower cost.
