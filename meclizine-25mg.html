About Meclizine HCl 25mg (30ct) — Complete Product Guide

Meclizine HCl 25mg is an antihistamine used to prevent and treat nausea, vomiting, and dizziness caused by motion sickness. It is also used to relieve symptoms of vertigo associated with inner ear disorders. The active ingredient in Bonine and Dramamine Less Drowsy Formula, meclizine provides effective motion sickness prevention with less sedation than older antihistamines like dimenhydrinate.

What Conditions Does Meclizine 25mg Treat?

Meclizine 25mg is indicated for the relief of prevention and treatment of motion sickness (car, boat, plane, train), vertigo and dizziness from inner ear disorders (benign paroxysmal positional vertigo, labyrinthitis, Meniere's disease), nausea and vomiting associated with vestibular disturbance. It is approved for OTC use and does not require a prescription for standard adult doses.

How Does Meclizine 25mg Work?

Meclizine works by blocking H1 histamine receptors in the brain — particularly in the vomiting center and the vestibular pathways that regulate balance and motion perception. By reducing vestibular stimulation signaling, it prevents the nausea and dizziness that occur when the brain receives conflicting motion signals from the eyes and inner ear. It also has mild anticholinergic properties that contribute to its anti-emetic effects. Onset is 1 hour; duration 8–24 hours.

Dosage and Usage Instructions

For motion sickness prevention: Take 25–50mg (1–2 tablets) 1 hour before travel begins. May repeat every 24 hours during travel. For vertigo: 25–100mg per day in divided doses as directed by your physician. Adults and children 12 and over only. Do not exceed 100mg in 24 hours. The 30-count pack provides 30 doses of motion sickness prevention.

Who Can Use Meclizine 25mg?

Meclizine 25mg OTC is for adults and children 12 and older. Avoid in patients with glaucoma, enlarged prostate, or difficulty urinating. Use with caution in patients with liver disease. Not recommended during pregnancy without medical advice — consult your OB/GYN. Consult your doctor if vertigo is severe, persistent, or accompanied by hearing loss or severe headache.

Possible Side Effects

Drowsiness is the most common side effect, though less pronounced than with dimenhydrinate (regular Dramamine). Other effects include dry mouth, blurred vision, and urinary retention. Avoid alcohol and other sedatives when taking meclizine. Use caution when driving or operating machinery. Elderly patients are more sensitive to anticholinergic side effects.
