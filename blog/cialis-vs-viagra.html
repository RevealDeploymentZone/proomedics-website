Cialis vs Viagra: Which ED Medication is Right for You?

By Promedic Medical Team  |  July 13, 2026  |  8 min read

Erectile dysfunction (ED) affects over 30 million men in the United States, and choosing the right medication can feel overwhelming. The two most well-known treatments — Cialis (tadalafil) and Viagra (sildenafil) — are both highly effective, but they work differently and suit different lifestyles. In this comprehensive guide, we break down everything you need to know to make an informed choice.

Key Takeaway: Viagra works fast (30–60 min) and lasts 4–6 hours — ideal for planned encounters. Cialis lasts up to 36 hours, giving you more spontaneity. Both are equally effective when taken correctly.

What is Viagra (Sildenafil)?

Viagra, whose active ingredient is sildenafil citrate, was the first FDA-approved oral treatment for erectile dysfunction, approved in 1998. It belongs to a class of drugs called PDE5 inhibitors, which work by relaxing the blood vessels in the penis to allow increased blood flow during sexual stimulation.

Viagra typically comes in 25mg, 50mg, and 100mg tablets. The standard recommended dose is 50mg, taken approximately 30 to 60 minutes before sexual activity. According to WebMD, sildenafil is effective in approximately 70% of men with ED.

How Viagra Works

When you are sexually aroused, your body releases nitric oxide, which triggers the production of cyclic GMP (cGMP). This chemical relaxes the smooth muscles in the penis, allowing blood to flow in and create an erection. The enzyme PDE5 normally breaks down cGMP, but sildenafil blocks PDE5, allowing cGMP to accumulate and sustain the erection.

What is Cialis (Tadalafil)?

Cialis, containing tadalafil, is often called "the weekend pill" because its effects last up to 36 hours — far longer than any other ED medication. Approved by the FDA in 2003, Cialis is available in 5mg, 10mg, and 20mg doses.

Tadalafil can be taken as needed (10mg or 20mg) or as a daily low dose (2.5mg or 5mg) for men who anticipate sexual activity several times a week. The Mayo Clinic notes that tadalafil's extended duration makes it unique among ED medications.

How Cialis Works

Like Viagra, Cialis is a PDE5 inhibitor. However, tadalafil has a much longer half-life (17.5 hours vs. 4 hours for sildenafil), which explains why it remains effective for up to 36 hours. This makes Cialis a popular choice for men who want flexibility without planning sex around a pill schedule.

Side Effects: Cialis vs Viagra

Both medications share some common side effects since they work through the same mechanism. According to the FDA, the most commonly reported side effects include:

  • Headache — most common with both medications
  • Flushing (warmth or redness in the face)
  • Nasal congestion
  • Upset stomach
  • Dizziness

Viagra is more likely to cause vision changes (a bluish tinge or light sensitivity), while Cialis more commonly causes back pain and muscle aches due to tadalafil's effect on PDE11 enzymes in muscle tissue. These side effects are usually mild and temporary.

Who Should Choose Viagra?

Viagra is an excellent choice if you:

  • Prefer to take medication only when needed and know when you'll be sexually active
  • Want a well-established, widely researched medication
  • Have tried Cialis and experienced back pain
  • Are also taking a generic version like sildenafil for cost savings

Who Should Choose Cialis?

Cialis may be the better option if you:

  • Want spontaneity without planning sex around a medication window
  • Are sexually active more than twice a week (daily low-dose option)
  • Also have benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH)
  • Prefer not to worry about timing meals before taking your medication

What About Other ED Medications?

Beyond Viagra and Cialis, there are other effective options worth considering:

  • Levitra (vardenafil) — works similarly to Viagra with a slightly longer duration (5–8 hours)
  • Kamagra (sildenafil) — contains the same active ingredient as Viagra, popular as a cost-effective alternative
  • Spedra (avanafil) — the newest ED medication, works in as little as 15 minutes
  • Generic Sildenafil — identical to Viagra but at a much lower price

Important Safety Information

Both Cialis and Viagra are prescription medications and should be taken only under medical supervision. Do NOT take either medication if you:

  • Take nitrates for chest pain (e.g., nitroglycerin) — can cause dangerous blood pressure drop
  • Take riociguat (Adempas) for pulmonary hypertension
  • Have severe heart or liver disease
  • Have had a stroke or heart attack in the past 6 months

The NHS recommends speaking to a doctor before starting any ED medication to ensure it is safe for your individual health profile.
