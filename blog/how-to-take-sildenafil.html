How to Take Sildenafil for the Best Results

By Promedic Medical Team  |  July 16, 2026  |  7 min read

Taking sildenafil correctly makes a significant difference to how well it works. Many men are disappointed with their first experience because they did not allow enough time, ate the wrong foods, or were anxious. Here is a practical, evidence-based guide to getting the best results every time.

Key Takeaway: Take sildenafil 30-60 minutes before sex, on an empty stomach or after a light low-fat meal, limit alcohol, and ensure you are relaxed and sexually stimulated. These four factors have the biggest impact on effectiveness.

When to Take Sildenafil

Take sildenafil 30 to 60 minutes before sexual activity. Most men find 45-60 minutes optimal. The medication remains active for 4-6 hours, so there is no need to rush. Some men take it up to 4 hours before, according to WebMD, though beyond 6 hours effectiveness drops.

Food: What to Eat Before Taking Sildenafil

A high-fat meal — steak, burgers, cheese — can delay sildenafil absorption by 30-60 minutes by slowing gastric emptying. For fastest onset, take on an empty stomach (2-3 hours after your last meal). A light, low-fat meal (salad, grilled chicken, soup) is fine. Our guide on whether to take Viagra on an empty stomach covers this in full detail.

Correct Dosage

Sildenafil is available in 25mg, 50mg, and 100mg. The standard starting dose is 50mg. If well-tolerated but insufficient after several attempts, your doctor may increase to 100mg. Never exceed 100mg in 24 hours — the FDA guidance is clear that higher doses increase risk without improving efficacy.

Alcohol

One or two drinks is generally tolerable. Heavy drinking reduces effectiveness and raises blood pressure side effect risk. Read the full guide on alcohol and Viagra.

Mental State Matters

Sildenafil requires sexual stimulation — it does not produce erections automatically. Anxiety and performance pressure are among the most common reasons sildenafil appears to "fail." Creating a relaxed setting and communicating with your partner makes a real difference. If psychological factors are significant, see our article on stress, anxiety, and ED.

Give It Enough Attempts

Give sildenafil at least four to six attempts at optimal conditions before deciding it is not working. If after 8 attempts it consistently fails, consult a doctor — a higher dose or a different medication may suit you better.
