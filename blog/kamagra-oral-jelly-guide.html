Kamagra Oral Jelly: Complete Guide — How It Works, Dosage & Safety

By Promedic Medical Team  |  July 13, 2026  |  8 min read

Kamagra has become one of the most searched erectile dysfunction treatments worldwide, and Kamagra Oral Jelly in particular attracts significant interest due to its unique form, faster onset, and range of flavors. But what exactly is Kamagra? How does it compare to Viagra? And is it safe to use? This comprehensive guide answers all your questions.

Key Takeaway: Kamagra contains sildenafil citrate — the same active ingredient as Viagra. Kamagra Oral Jelly is absorbed faster than tablets (onset in 15–30 minutes) and is popular with men who have difficulty swallowing pills.

What is Kamagra?

Kamagra is manufactured by Ajanta Pharma, an Indian pharmaceutical company. It was developed as a more affordable alternative to brand-name Viagra for international markets. The active ingredient in Kamagra is sildenafil citrate — the same molecule found in Viagra and generic sildenafil tablets.

Kamagra is available in several forms:

  • Kamagra Tablets — 100mg sildenafil, similar format to Viagra
  • Kamagra Oral Jelly — 100mg sildenafil in a flavored gel sachet
  • Kamagra Chewable Tablets — 100mg sildenafil in a chewable format
  • Kamagra Effervescent — dissolves in water for a fizzy drink format

How Does Kamagra Oral Jelly Work?

Kamagra Oral Jelly works through the same mechanism as all PDE5 inhibitors. When you are sexually stimulated, your body releases nitric oxide, which triggers the production of cyclic GMP (cGMP). This chemical relaxes the smooth muscle tissue in the penile blood vessels, allowing blood to flow in and create an erection.

Sildenafil (the active ingredient in Kamagra) blocks the PDE5 enzyme that would normally break down cGMP. By inhibiting PDE5, Kamagra allows cGMP to accumulate, making it easier to achieve and maintain an erection in response to sexual stimulation.

According to the National Library of Medicine, sildenafil has a well-established safety and efficacy profile with over two decades of clinical use worldwide.

Why Does Kamagra Oral Jelly Work Faster?

The oral jelly format is absorbed through the mucous membranes in the mouth and stomach more rapidly than a compressed tablet. This bypasses some of the first-pass metabolism in the liver, resulting in a faster rise in blood concentration of sildenafil. Most users report effects beginning within 15 to 30 minutes — significantly faster than the 30–60 minutes typical of standard sildenafil tablets.

Kamagra Flavors

One of the most appealing aspects of Kamagra Oral Jelly is its range of fruit flavors, which makes it more pleasant to take compared to swallowing a tablet. Common available flavors include:

  • Strawberry
  • Mango
  • Pineapple
  • Orange
  • Caramel
  • Banana
  • Vanilla

Each sachet typically comes in a 5g squeeze pack. The jelly can be consumed directly from the sachet or mixed into a non-alcoholic drink.

Dosage and How to Take Kamagra Oral Jelly

The standard dose of Kamagra Oral Jelly is one 100mg sachet per day. It should be taken approximately 15–30 minutes before anticipated sexual activity. Important dosage guidelines include:

  • Never exceed one sachet (100mg) in a 24-hour period
  • Take on an empty stomach or after a light, low-fat meal for best results
  • Avoid alcohol or limit to 1–2 standard drinks
  • Do not take with grapefruit juice (inhibits metabolism)
  • Sexual stimulation is required for the medication to work

If you are new to sildenafil, starting with a lower dose (half a sachet, approximately 50mg) is advisable to assess tolerance and side effects.

Kamagra vs Viagra: Key Differences

Since both Kamagra and Viagra contain sildenafil citrate, their core effectiveness is identical. The main differences are:

  • Form: Kamagra offers oral jelly and chewable formats not available with standard Viagra
  • Price: Kamagra is typically more affordable than brand Viagra
  • Onset: Kamagra Oral Jelly may work slightly faster than Viagra tablets
  • Brand recognition: Viagra has the longer FDA history and broader clinical data

Side Effects of Kamagra

As with all sildenafil products, potential side effects of Kamagra include:

  • Headache (most common)
  • Facial flushing
  • Nasal congestion
  • Indigestion or stomach upset
  • Dizziness
  • Temporary visual disturbances (rare)

Serious side effects are rare but require immediate medical attention: sudden vision or hearing loss, chest pain, or erections lasting more than 4 hours (priapism). The NHS sildenafil guidance provides a full list of potential side effects and when to seek help.

Who Should Not Take Kamagra?

Kamagra (like all sildenafil products) is contraindicated for men who:

  • Take nitrate medications for chest pain or heart conditions
  • Take riociguat (Adempas) for pulmonary arterial hypertension
  • Have recently had a stroke, heart attack, or life-threatening arrhythmia
  • Have severe liver or kidney disease
  • Have a history of priapism (prolonged erections)
  • Have retinitis pigmentosa (inherited eye disease)
