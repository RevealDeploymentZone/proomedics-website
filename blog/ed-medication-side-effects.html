ED Medication Side Effects: What to Expect and How to Manage Them

By Promedic Medical Team  |  July 22, 2026  |  9 min read

Starting an ED medication for the first time can be daunting — particularly if you are worried about side effects. The good news is that for the majority of men, side effects from PDE5 inhibitors like Viagra (sildenafil) and Cialis (tadalafil) are mild, temporary, and manageable. This comprehensive guide covers all the known side effects of every major ED medication, how common they are, who is most at risk, and practical strategies to minimise them.

Key Takeaway: The most common ED medication side effects — headache, flushing, nasal congestion — affect a minority of users, are typically mild, and resolve within 2–4 hours. Serious side effects are rare. Understanding what to expect helps you use these medications safely and confidently.

How ED Medications Work and Why They Cause Side Effects

All currently available oral ED medications belong to the PDE5 inhibitor class. They work by blocking the phosphodiesterase type 5 (PDE5) enzyme, which allows blood vessels to relax and blood to flow more freely — not just in the penis, but to some degree throughout the body. This systemic effect is the source of most side effects.

Because PDE5 enzymes are also present in blood vessels of the face, nasal passages, and other tissues, the medication can cause vasodilation (widening of blood vessels) in these areas too — hence headache, flushing, and nasal congestion. The FDA has compiled comprehensive safety data from clinical trials for all approved ED drugs.

Side Effects Specific to Each Medication

Sildenafil (Viagra / Generic)

Sildenafil is the most studied ED medication with over 25 years of safety data. Its most distinctive side effects are:

  • Visual disturbances — a temporary bluish-green tinge or increased light sensitivity, occurring in around 3% of users at the 100mg dose. This is caused by mild inhibition of PDE6 enzymes in the retina and is dose-dependent and reversible.
  • Flushing — more pronounced than with tadalafil due to sildenafil's higher peak blood concentrations
  • Food sensitivity — high-fat meals delay absorption by up to 60 minutes. Read: Viagra on an Empty Stomach: Timing Tips

Tadalafil (Cialis / Generic)

Tadalafil's unique side effect profile stems from its longer half-life and effect on PDE11 enzymes:

  • Back pain and muscle aches — the most distinctive tadalafil side effect, occurring in approximately 6% of users, typically 12–24 hours after dosing and resolving within 48 hours
  • Indigestion — slightly more common with tadalafil than other ED drugs
  • Visual disturbances are significantly rarer than with sildenafil

Vardenafil (Levitra)

Levitra (vardenafil) has a similar profile to sildenafil, with slightly more nasal congestion but fewer visual effects:

  • Higher rates of nasal congestion (9%) compared to sildenafil and tadalafil
  • May cause a mild increase in QTc interval on ECG — discuss with your cardiologist if you have any cardiac arrhythmias

Avanafil (Spedra)

Spedra (avanafil) is the newest ED medication and is designed to be more PDE5-selective, resulting in fewer off-target side effects:

  • Lower rates of headache (12%) and flushing (6%) than older agents
  • Minimal vision effects due to very low PDE6 inhibition
  • No significant back pain risk (low PDE11 activity)

Serious But Rare Side Effects: When to Seek Immediate Help

Seek emergency medical attention immediately if you experience:
  • Priapism — a painful erection lasting more than 4 hours. This is a medical emergency that can cause permanent penile damage if untreated. Go to an emergency room immediately.
  • Sudden vision loss in one or both eyes — may indicate NAION (non-arteritic anterior ischemic optic neuropathy). Extremely rare but serious.
  • Sudden hearing loss or ringing in the ears
  • Chest pain during or after sexual activity — stop activity immediately, rest, and call 911
  • Severe dizziness, fainting — especially if combined with nitrates

According to the NHS, serious side effects are extremely rare when ED medications are used correctly, at appropriate doses, and without contraindicated drug combinations.

Practical Tips to Minimise Side Effects

  1. Start with the lowest effective dose — many men find that 25mg sildenafil or 10mg tadalafil works just as well as higher doses with fewer side effects. Read: How to Take Sildenafil for Best Results
  2. Avoid high-fat meals before sildenafil — food slows absorption and can intensify stomach-related side effects. Tadalafil and avanafil are less food-sensitive.
  3. Limit alcohol — alcohol amplifies vasodilation, worsening headache and flushing, and increases dizziness. Limit to 1–2 drinks. Read: Does Alcohol Affect Viagra?
  4. Stay hydrated — dehydration worsens headaches. Drinking a glass of water with your medication can help.
  5. Take the medication consistently — for daily tadalafil, side effects typically diminish over the first 2–3 weeks as the body adapts.
  6. Try a different medication — if one ED drug causes intolerable side effects, switching to another often resolves the issue. For example, switching from sildenafil to avanafil can reduce visual and flushing side effects. Read: Sildenafil vs Tadalafil: Which is Better?

Who is Most at Risk of Side Effects?

Most men tolerate ED medications well, but certain factors increase side effect risk:

  • Men over 65 — slower drug clearance means higher peak blood concentrations; lower starting doses are often recommended
  • Liver or kidney impairment — affects drug metabolism; dose adjustment may be required
  • Men taking alpha-blockers or antihypertensives — additive blood pressure lowering
  • Men on CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g., ketoconazole, ritonavir) — significantly increase ED drug blood levels

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most common side effects of Viagra?

The most common side effects of Viagra (sildenafil) are headache (16%), flushing (10%), nasal congestion (4%), and indigestion (7%). Visual disturbances such as a bluish tinge occur in less than 3% of users and are dose-dependent and temporary.

Do ED medication side effects go away?

Yes — for most men, ED medication side effects are mild, temporary, and improve with repeated use as the body adjusts. Headache and flushing typically resolve within 2–4 hours of taking the medication. With daily tadalafil, side effects often diminish significantly after the first 2–3 weeks.

How do I reduce ED medication side effects?

Take the lowest effective dose, avoid high-fat meals before sildenafil, limit alcohol to 1–2 drinks, stay well-hydrated, and try to take the medication at the same time consistently. If side effects persist, switching to a different ED medication — such as from sildenafil to avanafil — often helps.

Which ED medication has the fewest side effects?

Avanafil (Spedra) is generally considered to have the most favourable side effect profile due to its high PDE5 selectivity — lower rates of headache, flushing, visual disturbances, and back pain compared to older agents. Daily low-dose tadalafil also tends to produce fewer acute side effects than higher on-demand doses.

Conclusion

ED medication side effects are real, but for the vast majority of men they are mild, short-lived, and easy to manage with a few simple strategies. Understanding which side effects to expect from each medication — and knowing the rare serious warning signs — empowers you to use these treatments safely and effectively.
