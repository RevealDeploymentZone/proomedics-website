About Viagra

Viagra is the brand name for Sildenafil Citrate, developed by Pfizer. It was the first oral medication approved for treating erectile dysfunction and has been helping men since 1998. Viagra works by increasing blood flow to the penis, enabling men to achieve and maintain an erection when sexually aroused.

How Viagra Works

Sildenafil, the active ingredient in Viagra, belongs to a class of medications called PDE5 inhibitors. It works by relaxing blood vessels in the penis, allowing increased blood flow during sexual stimulation. The medication typically takes 30-60 minutes to work and remains effective for 4-6 hours.

Who Can Take Viagra?

Viagra is suitable for adult men over 18 experiencing erectile dysfunction. However, it may not be appropriate for everyone. Consult a healthcare professional before use, especially if you:

  • Have heart or cardiovascular problems
  • Have low or high blood pressure
  • Have had a stroke or heart attack
  • Have liver or kidney disease
  • Take nitrate medications

Dosage and Administration

The recommended starting dose is 50mg, taken approximately one hour before sexual activity. Based on effectiveness and tolerance, the dose may be increased to 100mg or decreased to 25mg. Do not take more than one dose per day.

Possible Side Effects

Most men tolerate Viagra well. Common side effects may include:

  • Headache
  • Facial flushing
  • Indigestion
  • Nasal congestion
  • Dizziness

These side effects are usually mild and temporary. Seek immediate medical attention if you experience vision changes, chest pain, or an erection lasting more than 4 hours.
