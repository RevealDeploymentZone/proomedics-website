About Viagra (Sildenafil Citrate)

Viagra is the pioneering erectile dysfunction treatment developed by Pfizer Pharmaceuticals. Containing the active ingredient Sildenafil Citrate, Viagra revolutionized ED treatment when it was approved by the FDA in 1998. Since then, it has helped over 60 million men worldwide overcome erectile difficulties and restore confidence in their sexual lives.

As the first oral PDE5 inhibitor, Viagra set the standard for ED medications. Its proven track record, extensive clinical research, and widespread medical acceptance make it the most prescribed erectile dysfunction treatment globally.

How Viagra Works

Sildenafil Citrate, the active pharmaceutical ingredient in Viagra, belongs to a class of medications called phosphodiesterase type 5 (PDE5) inhibitors. The mechanism of action involves:

  • Enzyme Inhibition: Sildenafil blocks PDE5, an enzyme that regulates blood flow in the penis
  • Vasodilation: This inhibition allows smooth muscles in blood vessel walls to relax
  • Increased Blood Flow: Relaxed vessels enable greater blood flow to erectile tissue
  • Natural Response: Effect only occurs with sexual arousal - no spontaneous erections

Viagra typically begins working within 30-60 minutes of ingestion and remains effective for approximately 4-6 hours. The medication requires sexual stimulation to produce an erection, ensuring a natural response rather than an artificial one.

Who Can Take Viagra?

Viagra is suitable for adult men over 18 experiencing erectile dysfunction. However, medical consultation is essential before use. Viagra may not be appropriate if you:

  • Have severe cardiovascular disease or recent heart attack/stroke (within 6 months)
  • Have low blood pressure (below 90/50 mmHg) or uncontrolled high blood pressure
  • Have severe liver or kidney impairment
  • Have hereditary degenerative retinal disorders
  • Take nitrate medications for angina (dangerous interaction)
  • Take riociguat for pulmonary hypertension
  • Are allergic to Sildenafil or any excipients

Dosage and Administration

Recommended Starting Dose: 50mg taken approximately one hour before anticipated sexual activity

Dose Adjustment: Based on efficacy and tolerability, dose may be increased to 100mg or decreased to 25mg

Maximum Frequency: One dose per 24-hour period

Optimal Timing: Take 30-60 minutes before sexual activity on an empty stomach for fastest absorption

Food Interaction: High-fat meals may delay onset - take on empty stomach or after light meal

Possible Side Effects

Most men tolerate Viagra well. Common side effects (affecting 1-10% of users) include:

  • Headache - most frequent, usually mild, resolves within hours
  • Facial Flushing - temporary redness due to increased blood flow
  • Indigestion - mild gastric discomfort
  • Nasal Congestion - stuffy nose
  • Dizziness - mild, especially when standing quickly
  • Visual Disturbances - rare, temporary blue tinge or light sensitivity

Rare but Serious Side Effects:

  • Priapism: Erection lasting over 4 hours requires immediate medical attention
  • Sudden Vision Loss: Very rare, seek immediate medical care
  • Sudden Hearing Loss: Rare, discontinue and consult doctor
  • Chest Pain: Stop sexual activity and seek medical help

Drug Interactions

Contraindicated (Never combine with):

  • Nitrates (nitroglycerin, isosorbide) - can cause dangerous blood pressure drop
  • Riociguat - severe hypotension risk
  • Recreational drugs containing nitrites (poppers)

Use with Caution:

  • Alpha-blockers for prostate - may need dose adjustment
  • Other blood pressure medications - monitor blood pressure
  • Antifungals (ketoconazole, itraconazole) - may increase Sildenafil levels
  • HIV protease inhibitors - dose reduction may be necessary

Tips for Best Results

  • Take on an empty stomach or after light meal for faster absorption
  • Avoid excessive alcohol (more than 2 units) which can impair effectiveness
  • Allow 30-60 minutes before sexual activity
  • Sexual stimulation is necessary - Viagra doesn't create automatic erections
  • Avoid grapefruit juice which can increase side effects
  • Stay hydrated to minimize headache risk
  • Store at room temperature away from moisture and heat
