About Loratadine 10mg (30ct) — Complete Product Guide

Loratadine 10mg is a second-generation antihistamine used to relieve symptoms of seasonal and year-round allergic rhinitis (hay fever) and hives (urticaria). It is the active ingredient in Claritin. Unlike first-generation antihistamines (such as diphenhydramine), loratadine is non-drowsy at standard doses because it does not cross the blood-brain barrier to the same degree.

What Conditions Does Loratadine 10mg Treat?

Loratadine 10mg is indicated for the relief of seasonal allergic rhinitis (hay fever), perennial allergic rhinitis (year-round allergies), pet dander allergies, dust mite and mold allergies, pollen allergies, allergic conjunctivitis (itchy/watery eyes), and chronic urticaria (hives). It is approved for OTC use and does not require a prescription for standard adult doses.

How Does Loratadine 10mg Work?

Loratadine works by selectively blocking peripheral H1 histamine receptors — the receptors that trigger allergy symptoms such as sneezing, itching, runny nose, and watery eyes. By preventing histamine from binding to these receptors, loratadine prevents and relieves allergy symptoms without causing significant sedation. One 10mg dose provides up to 24 hours of continuous relief.

Dosage and Usage Instructions

Adults and children 6 years and over: Take 1 tablet (10mg) once daily. Do not take more than 1 tablet per day. May be taken with or without food. For patients with liver or kidney impairment: dosing may need to be reduced — consult your doctor. The 30-count pack provides a full month's supply for continuous allergy control.

Who Can Use Loratadine 10mg?

Loratadine 10mg OTC is suitable for adults and children 6 years and older. Consult your doctor before use if you have liver or kidney disease. Generally safe during breastfeeding in usual doses — consult your physician. For children under 6, use children's loratadine syrup with weight-based dosing.

Possible Side Effects

Loratadine is very well-tolerated. Most common side effects include headache, dry mouth, and mild drowsiness (more likely at higher doses). Some people may experience fatigue or stomach pain. Serious allergic reactions are rare. Unlike first-generation antihistamines, loratadine does not typically impair driving ability or concentration.

Frequently Asked Questions — Loratadine 10mg

Does loratadine cause drowsiness?

Loratadine is classified as a non-drowsy antihistamine because it does not cross the blood-brain barrier as readily as older antihistamines like Diphenhydramine (Benadryl). A small percentage of people may still experience mild drowsiness. Source: MedlinePlus: Loratadine.

How long does loratadine 10mg last?

Loratadine 10mg provides 24-hour relief from allergy symptoms. You only need one tablet per day. It reaches peak effectiveness within 1–3 hours and maintains consistent coverage throughout the day, making it ideal for daily allergy management.

Can I take loratadine every day?

Yes — loratadine 10mg is safe for daily use and is specifically designed as a once-daily allergy medication. It can be taken continuously during allergy season or year-round for chronic allergies. Unlike older antihistamines, daily use does not significantly affect alertness or driving ability.

Loratadine vs Cetirizine — which is better?

Both are effective 24-hour non-drowsy antihistamines. Cetirizine (Zyrtec) may work slightly faster and be more effective for some people, but carries a slightly higher risk of drowsiness. Loratadine (Claritin) is the least sedating option and is better for people who need to stay fully alert.

What symptoms does loratadine treat?

Loratadine 10mg treats runny nose, sneezing, itchy/watery eyes, itchy nose and throat caused by seasonal hay fever (allergic rhinitis) and year-round indoor allergies. It also relieves hives (urticaria) and skin itching from allergic reactions.
