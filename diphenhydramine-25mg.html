About Diphenhydramine HCl 25mg (24ct) — Complete Product Guide

Diphenhydramine HCl 25mg is a first-generation antihistamine used both as an allergy reliever and an OTC sleep aid. It is the active ingredient in Benadryl and many nighttime cold and sleep products. Unlike second-generation antihistamines (cetirizine, loratadine), diphenhydramine causes significant drowsiness — a property that makes it effective for treating occasional sleeplessness alongside allergic reactions.

What Conditions Does Diphenhydramine 25mg Treat?

Diphenhydramine 25mg is indicated for the relief of acute allergic reactions, hay fever and seasonal allergies, hives and skin itching, allergic conjunctivitis, insect bite reactions, cold-related runny nose, motion sickness (mild), and occasional sleeplessness. It is approved for OTC use and does not require a prescription for standard adult doses.

How Does Diphenhydramine 25mg Work?

Diphenhydramine is a competitive H1 receptor antagonist that blocks both central and peripheral histamine receptors. Its ability to cross the blood-brain barrier causes sedation, making it effective for sleep induction. Peripherally, it relieves allergy symptoms including sneezing, runny nose, itching, and watery eyes. It also has mild anticholinergic effects, contributing to its drying properties for nasal symptoms. Onset of action is within 15–30 minutes.

Dosage and Usage Instructions

Adults and children 12 and over: Take 1–2 tablets (25–50mg) every 4–6 hours as needed for allergy. For sleep: take 2 tablets (50mg) at bedtime, 30 minutes before desired sleep time. Do not exceed 6 tablets (150mg) in 24 hours. Do not use for more than 2 weeks for sleep without a doctor's advice. Do not use with other sedatives or alcohol.

Who Can Use Diphenhydramine 25mg?

Diphenhydramine 25mg OTC is for adults and children 12 and older. Not recommended for children under 12 except under medical supervision. Avoid if you have glaucoma, enlarged prostate, difficulty urinating, or asthma. Elderly patients should use with caution due to increased fall risk and cognitive effects. Consult your doctor if pregnant or breastfeeding.

Possible Side Effects

Drowsiness is the most common and expected side effect. Other effects include dry mouth, blurred vision, urinary retention, constipation, and confusion (especially in elderly). Avoid driving or operating heavy machinery after use. Do not combine with alcohol, sedatives, or other CNS depressants. Elderly patients are more sensitive to anticholinergic effects.
