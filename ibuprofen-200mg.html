About Ibuprofen 200mg (100ct) — Complete Product Guide

Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to relieve pain, reduce fever, and decrease inflammation. It is the active ingredient found in popular brands like Advil and Motrin. Available over the counter in the US, Ibuprofen 200mg is the standard adult dose for mild to moderate pain management without requiring a prescription.

What Conditions Does Ibuprofen 200mg Treat?

Ibuprofen 200mg is indicated for the relief of headaches and migraines, muscle aches and soreness, back pain and lower back pain, arthritis and joint pain, menstrual cramps, dental pain, minor injuries, and fever reduction. It is approved for OTC use and does not require a prescription for standard adult doses.

How Does Ibuprofen 200mg Work?

Ibuprofen works by blocking the production of prostaglandins — natural chemicals in the body that trigger inflammation, pain, and fever. By inhibiting the COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes responsible for prostaglandin synthesis, Ibuprofen reduces swelling, lowers body temperature, and relieves pain signals. Effects typically begin within 20–30 minutes of ingestion and last 4–6 hours.

Dosage and Usage Instructions

Adults and children 12 years and older: Take 1 tablet (200mg) every 4 to 6 hours as needed. If pain or fever does not respond to 1 tablet, 2 tablets (400mg) may be used. Do not exceed 3 tablets (600mg) in 24 hours unless directed by a doctor. Take with food or milk to reduce stomach upset. Do not use for longer than 10 days for pain or 3 days for fever without consulting a healthcare provider.

Who Can Use Ibuprofen 200mg?

Ibuprofen 200mg OTC is suitable for adults and children 12 years and older. It is not recommended for people with a history of gastrointestinal bleeding, kidney disease, severe heart disease, or those taking blood thinners. Always consult a healthcare provider if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or on other medications.

Possible Side Effects

Ibuprofen is generally well-tolerated when used as directed. Common side effects may include stomach upset, nausea, heartburn, and mild headache. Taking it with food reduces GI discomfort. Avoid use if you have kidney disease, stomach ulcers, heart conditions, or are allergic to aspirin or NSAIDs. Not recommended for use during the third trimester of pregnancy.
