About Cialis (Tadalafil)

Cialis is a prescription medication containing Tadalafil, used to treat erectile dysfunction (ED) in men. Developed by Eli Lilly and FDA-approved in 2003, Cialis has become one of the most popular ED medications in America due to its unique long-lasting effect of up to 36 hours - earning it the nickname "the weekend pill."

Unlike other ED medications that last 4-6 hours, Cialis's extended duration provides unprecedented flexibility and spontaneity, allowing men to engage in sexual activity within a much wider time window after taking the medication.

How Cialis Works

Tadalafil, the active ingredient in Cialis, is a phosphodiesterase type 5 (PDE5) inhibitor. It works by:

  • Blocking PDE5 Enzyme: Prevents breakdown of cGMP, a substance that regulates blood flow
  • Relaxing Blood Vessels: Allows smooth muscles in penile arteries to relax
  • Increasing Blood Flow: Enhanced circulation to erectile tissue enables firmer erections
  • Extended Action: Unlike other ED meds, Cialis's effects last up to 36 hours
  • Natural Response: Requires sexual stimulation - no automatic erections

Cialis vs Viagra - Key Differences

  • Duration: Cialis lasts up to 36 hours vs Viagra's 4-6 hours
  • Onset Time: Both work in 30-60 minutes
  • Food Interaction: Cialis can be taken with food; Viagra best on empty stomach
  • Flexibility: Cialis provides greater spontaneity due to longer window
  • Daily Option: Cialis available as low-dose daily medication; Viagra is on-demand only
  • Alcohol: Moderate alcohol less likely to affect Cialis effectiveness

Who Can Take Cialis?

Cialis is FDA-approved for men over 18 experiencing erectile dysfunction. However, medical consultation is essential. Cialis may not be suitable if you:

  • Have severe cardiovascular disease or recent heart attack/stroke
  • Have low blood pressure (below 90/50 mmHg) or uncontrolled hypertension
  • Have severe liver or kidney impairment
  • Take nitrate medications for chest pain (dangerous interaction)
  • Take guanylate cyclase stimulators like riociguat
  • Have hereditary retinal disorders
  • Are allergic to Tadalafil or any ingredients

Possible Side Effects

Common side effects (affecting 1-10% of users):

  • Headache: Most common, usually mild and temporary
  • Indigestion: Upset stomach, heartburn
  • Back Pain: Muscle aches, typically resolves within 12-24 hours
  • Muscle Aches: Especially in legs and arms
  • Facial Flushing: Temporary redness
  • Nasal Congestion: Stuffy nose

Rare but Serious:

  • Priapism: Erection lasting over 4 hours - seek immediate help
  • Sudden Vision/Hearing Loss: Very rare, stop use and consult doctor
  • Chest Pain: Stop activity and seek medical attention

Drug Interactions

Never Combine With:

  • Nitrates (nitroglycerin, isosorbide) - can cause life-threatening blood pressure drop
  • Riociguat or other guanylate cyclase stimulators
  • Recreational drugs containing nitrites (poppers)

Use Caution With:

  • Alpha-blockers for prostate - may need dose adjustment and timing
  • Blood pressure medications - monitor BP closely
  • Antifungals (ketoconazole) - may increase Cialis levels
  • HIV protease inhibitors - dose reduction may be necessary
