About Cialis

Cialis is a prescription medication containing Tadalafil, used to treat erectile dysfunction. Known as "the weekend pill," Cialis offers the longest duration of action among ED treatments, remaining effective for up to 36 hours. This extended window provides greater spontaneity and flexibility compared to other ED medications.

How Cialis Works

Tadalafil, the active ingredient in Cialis, is a PDE5 inhibitor that enhances erectile function by increasing blood flow to the penis during sexual stimulation. Unlike shorter-acting medications, Cialis can work for up to 36 hours, allowing for multiple opportunities for sexual activity within that timeframe.

Cialis vs Viagra - Key Differences

  • Duration: Cialis lasts up to 36 hours vs Viagra's 4-6 hours
  • Onset: Cialis works in 30 minutes, can be taken with food
  • Flexibility: Greater spontaneity due to longer duration
  • Daily Option: Available as daily low-dose (5mg) for continuous readiness

Who Can Take Cialis?

Cialis is suitable for men over 18 experiencing erectile dysfunction. Consult a healthcare professional before use if you:

  • Have cardiovascular conditions
  • Have blood pressure issues
  • Have liver or kidney problems
  • Take nitrate medications or alpha-blockers
  • Have a history of priapism

Dosage and Administration

As-needed dosing: Take 10mg at least 30 minutes before sexual activity. May be increased to 20mg or decreased to 5mg based on response. Maximum once per day.

Daily dosing: Take 5mg once daily at the same time, regardless of sexual activity timing. Provides continuous readiness.

Possible Side Effects

Common side effects may include:

  • Headache
  • Indigestion or upset stomach
  • Back pain or muscle aches
  • Facial flushing
  • Nasal congestion

Side effects are usually mild and resolve within a few hours. Seek medical attention for persistent or severe symptoms.
