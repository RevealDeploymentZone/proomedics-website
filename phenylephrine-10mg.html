About Phenylephrine HCl 10mg Tablets (24ct) — Complete Product Guide

Phenylephrine HCl 10mg is an OTC nasal decongestant used to temporarily relieve nasal congestion, sinus pressure, and stuffy nose caused by the common cold, hay fever, upper respiratory allergies, and sinusitis. It is the active ingredient in Sudafed PE and is available without ID requirements (unlike pseudoephedrine-based decongestants). Phenylephrine works as a sympathomimetic agent that constricts nasal blood vessels.

What Conditions Does Phenylephrine 10mg Treat?

Phenylephrine 10mg is indicated for the relief of nasal congestion from colds and upper respiratory infections, sinus congestion and pressure, allergic rhinitis-related stuffy nose, acute sinusitis, eustachian tube congestion, and nasal swelling. It is approved for OTC use and does not require a prescription for standard adult doses.

How Does Phenylephrine 10mg Work?

Phenylephrine is an alpha-1 adrenergic receptor agonist. By stimulating alpha-1 receptors in the nasal mucosa, it causes vasoconstriction — narrowing the swollen blood vessels lining the nasal passages. This reduces blood flow to the nasal tissue, decreasing swelling and congestion. The result is improved airflow through the nasal passages and reduced sinus pressure. Onset occurs within 15–30 minutes; duration is approximately 4 hours.

Dosage and Usage Instructions

Adults and children 12 and over: Take 1 tablet (10mg) every 4 hours as needed. Do not exceed 6 tablets (60mg) in 24 hours. Do not use for more than 7 days. Do not crush or chew tablets. May be taken with or without food. If sinus pain or pressure is also present, consider a combination product with an analgesic.

Who Can Use Phenylephrine 10mg?

Phenylephrine 10mg OTC is for adults and children 12 and older. Not recommended for people with high blood pressure, heart disease, thyroid disease, diabetes, or prostate problems without doctor approval — the sympathomimetic effect may worsen these conditions. Avoid if you take MAOIs or tricyclic antidepressants. Consult your doctor if pregnant or breastfeeding.

Possible Side Effects

Common side effects include nervousness, restlessness, headache, and increased blood pressure. Some users experience rapid or irregular heartbeat, dizziness, or insomnia. These effects are more common at higher doses. Avoid taking close to bedtime. Do not use with MAOIs or other decongestants. Stop use if heart palpitations occur.

Frequently Asked Questions — Phenylephrine 10mg

How does phenylephrine work as a decongestant?

Phenylephrine is a sympathomimetic decongestant that works by constricting blood vessels in the nasal passages, reducing swelling and congestion. This opens the nasal airways for easier breathing. Effects begin within 30–60 minutes. Source: MedlinePlus: Phenylephrine.

How effective is phenylephrine vs pseudoephedrine?

Pseudoephedrine (behind-the-counter) has consistently shown stronger decongestant effects in clinical studies. In 2023, the FDA advisory panel concluded that oral phenylephrine is not effective as a nasal decongestant at OTC doses. Pseudoephedrine remains the gold standard oral decongestant — ask your pharmacist.

Can I take phenylephrine with blood pressure medication?

Use with caution — phenylephrine can raise blood pressure. It is generally not recommended for people with hypertension, heart disease, thyroid disease, or diabetes without consulting a doctor first. See FDA prescribing information.

How often can I take phenylephrine 10mg?

Adults: Take 1 tablet (10mg) every 4 hours as needed. Do not exceed 6 doses (60mg) in 24 hours. Do not use for more than 7 days. If nasal congestion does not improve, see a doctor — you may have a bacterial sinusitis requiring antibiotics.

What is the difference between phenylephrine and oxymetazoline (Afrin nasal spray)?

Oxymetazoline (Afrin) is a topical nasal spray that works faster (within minutes) and is more potent than oral phenylephrine. However, Afrin should not be used for more than 3 consecutive days due to rebound congestion. Oral phenylephrine is better for longer-term management of nasal congestion.
