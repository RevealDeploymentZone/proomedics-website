About Cetirizine HCl 10mg (45ct) — Complete Product Guide

Cetirizine HCl 10mg is a second-generation antihistamine used to relieve allergy symptoms. It is the active ingredient in Zyrtec. Cetirizine is known for working faster than loratadine and providing powerful 24-hour allergy relief. While technically classified as low-sedation, a small percentage of users may experience mild drowsiness, making it popular for evening use as well.

What Conditions Does Cetirizine 10mg Treat?

Cetirizine 10mg is indicated for the relief of seasonal allergic rhinitis (hay fever), perennial allergic rhinitis (year-round indoor allergies), allergic conjunctivitis, pet dander and dust mite allergies, pollen and mold spore allergies, chronic urticaria (hives), and itching from allergic skin reactions. It is approved for OTC use and does not require a prescription for standard adult doses.

How Does Cetirizine 10mg Work?

Cetirizine is a highly selective H1 receptor antagonist — it blocks histamine receptors in the body's tissues to prevent allergy symptoms from occurring. It has a longer binding affinity to H1 receptors compared to loratadine, contributing to its reputation for stronger and faster-acting relief. It is also mast cell stabilizing, which helps prevent the cascade of allergy reactions before they start.

Dosage and Usage Instructions

Adults and children 6 years and over: Take 1 tablet (10mg) once daily. May be taken in the morning or evening — evening dosing is preferred by users who experience mild drowsiness. Take with or without food. Do not exceed 1 tablet per day. The 45-count pack provides a 45-day continuous supply.

Who Can Use Cetirizine 10mg?

Cetirizine 10mg OTC is for adults and children 6 years and older. Patients with kidney impairment may need a lower dose (5mg once daily) — consult your doctor. Generally considered safe during pregnancy at standard doses — always check with your OB. Not recommended during breastfeeding due to potential infant drowsiness.

Possible Side Effects

Cetirizine may cause mild drowsiness in some people (more than loratadine but less than first-generation antihistamines). Other common side effects include dry mouth, headache, and fatigue. Most people tolerate it very well. Use caution when driving or operating machinery until you know how cetirizine affects you. Avoid alcohol.

Frequently Asked Questions — Cetirizine 10mg

How quickly does cetirizine work?

Cetirizine 10mg begins working within 1 hour of taking it, with maximum effect at 1–2 hours. It provides 24-hour relief with a single daily dose. For best results, take at the same time each day. Source: MedlinePlus: Cetirizine.

Does cetirizine cause drowsiness?

Cetirizine is considered low-sedating but more likely to cause drowsiness than loratadine. About 10–14% of users experience some drowsiness. If you find it sedating, take it at night. Compare with Loratadine (Claritin) — the least sedating option.

Can I take cetirizine with other medications?

Cetirizine interacts with central nervous system depressants (alcohol, sedatives, sleep aids) — avoid combining. It also interacts with theophylline. Check with your pharmacist before combining with prescription medications. See FDA drug interaction resources.

Is cetirizine the same as Zyrtec?

Yes — Zyrtec is the brand name and cetirizine is the generic active ingredient. Generic cetirizine 10mg is bioequivalent to Zyrtec 10mg and provides the same 24-hour allergy relief at a significantly lower cost.

Can children take cetirizine 10mg?

The 10mg dose is approved for adults and children 6 years and older. Children aged 2–5 years may use a lower 5mg dose (syrup or half tablet) under medical supervision. Always follow package directions or consult a pediatrician.
